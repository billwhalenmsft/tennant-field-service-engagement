1 The problem we set out to solve

Tennant runs a global field-service operation — ~500 EMEA technicians, 3–4 jobs a day, often offline — on a fragmented regional ERP landscape. The cost shows up as lost technician productivity, avoidable truck rolls, and slow, manual coordination. EMEA is the first rollout.

~500 EMEA techs, often offline

Paperwork + lost productivity → offline-capable mobile with Copilot-assisted task completion.

Avoidable truck rolls

Cost + equipment downtime → self-service portal + remote IoT command for deflection.

Connected equipment (X4 ROVR, T600E)

Telemetry underused → IoT signals auto-raise the case and the work order before the customer calls.

Fragmented regional ERP

SAP in NA, Vitec/Navision/Tally regionally → a service spine that sits above, integrating per region in phases.

2 What we showed — one closed loop

A single linear thread, end to end. The anchor: "all roads lead to a work order." Built on out-of-the-box Dynamics 365 Field Service + Customer Service, with AI, IoT, and self-service on top.

IoT signal→ Case→ Work Order→ Schedule / Dispatch→ Technician mobile (offline)→ Asset 360 / Optimize→ Lead to Sales

The hero scenario: an X4 ROVR autonomous scrubber battery replacement at a UK distribution site — a reactive case and a proactive, contract-driven preventive work order, scheduled, dispatched, and executed on a technician's offline mobile, with the asset's full 360 history and a follow-on sales lead — all in one connected flow.

5 Proposed next phase

The EMEA service spine is the foundation. The natural next step is a focused proof of value on the highest-friction integration point.

Regional ERP proof of value. Take one region's ERP (e.g. the EMEA SAP or a regional system) and prove the work-order-to-ERP round trip — so service execution, parts, and billing stay in sync across the fragmented landscape. Scope it small, prove it real, then template it region by region.
